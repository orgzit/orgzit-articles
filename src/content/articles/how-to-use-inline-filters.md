Using [Filters](/articles/how-to-filter-data-in-orgzit), you can view records that match pre-defined values and conditions. Orgzit also provides a feature to [save a filter](/articles/how-do-saved-filters-work), for later use.

Filters can help you if:

1.  You want to look at data entries created before/after a particular date.
    
2.  You want to look at values that meet specific criteria. Eg. sales orders with _amount > 10,000_ or sales orders with _status = delivered_.
    
3.  You want to look at data that meet multiple conditions like the ones mentioned above.
    

**Inline Filters and its Benefits**

*   Inline Filters help you to apply filters on multiple fields on one go.
    
*   Using Inline Filters, you can apply filter even if the column is not visible in the [tabular view](/articles/tabular-view-in-orgzit).
    
*   Inline Filter works in non-tabular views ([kanban](/articles/kanban-view-in-orgzit), [calendar](/articles/calendar-view), [map](/articles/map-view-in-orgzit)).
    

You can apply inline filters by clicking on the filter option at the top of your Table.

![](/images/how-to-use-inline-filters/img-1.png)

You can set the required values and conditions there and **apply** the Filter.

![](/images/how-to-use-inline-filters/img-2.png)

Once you apply a filter, you can save it for the future by clicking on the [Saved Filters](/articles/how-do-saved-filters-work) button. You can name the Filter and save it in your list of Saved Filters.

![](/images/how-to-use-inline-filters/img-3.png)
